IT MATTERS

Every login page is a decision point.

Banking apps, e-wallets, email, social platforms, and online shopping accounts all share the same weak point: a login page. Copy one convincingly enough, and most people only notice something's wrong after they've typed a password into it.

Banking & e-wallets

Where a stolen session turns into a stolen balance.

Email

The front door attackers use to reach every other account.

Social media

Identity theft with a follower count attached.

E-commerce

Checkout pages built to steal, not to sell.

HOW DETECTION WORKS

One visit. Three signals. One verdict.

Phishield pulls features from the URL, the page's DOM structure, and the login form itself, funnels them through an XGBoost model exported to ONNX, and runs the whole thing on-device so page content doesn't need to leave the browser to get a verdict.

Inference runs locally in the browser via ONNX Runtime Web. The trusted-domain list and model are synced periodically, but scoring a page doesn't require sending its contents anywhere.
EXPLAINABLE, NOT A BLACK BOX

Every warning shows its work.

A verdict alone doesn't teach anyone to spot the next one. Phishield's explainability module breaks its own decision into plain-language reasons the same way a security analyst would talk through a red flag surfaced in the warning overlay, the browser indicator, and the dashboard.

    MEASURING WHAT MATTERS

    Evaluated like a security tool, not a demo.

    Detection performance is assessed with four standard classification metrics, alongside a separate software-quality evaluation.

    Accuracy

    How often the overall verdict safe or phishing is correct.

    Precision

    Of the pages flagged as phishing, how many actually were.

    Recall

    Of the real phishing pages, how many Phishield actually caught.

    F1-score

    The balance between catching threats and avoiding false alarms.

    Evaluated with two respondent groups: everyday end users, and system evaluators drawn from IT and cybersecurity practitioners.
